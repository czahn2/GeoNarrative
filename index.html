Airports Across the Globe

by Camille Zahn

Airports are vital transportation hubs that facilitate global connectivity and economic growth. Airports enable swift movement of goods and people, connecting businesses with suppliers and customers across the globe. Tourism is also closely linked with air travel, as many travelers' destinations are determined by the flights available and the accessibility of a particular desination. Airports are also significant employers - there are airport staff, air traffic controllers, security personnel, as well as pilots and flight attendants. These facilities often involve state-of-the-art infrastrucutre to help organize the mass amounts of people, baggage, and flights, serving as a symbol for an area's prosperity. In summary, airports are vital to global progress, and nations without strong aviation infrastructure face challenges related to economic growth, accessibility, and international engagement.

Airport Organization in the US

As of 2021, there are approximately 20,000 airports in the United States of America, including both public and private facilities. The vast majority of the United States' 3,100 counties have at least one airport, which vary widely in purpose and type. Major metropolitan areas tend to have one or multiple large international airports that serve both commercial aviation passengers and cargo and trade companies. Rural counties sometimes have small regional airports that can connect people and goods to larger cities and the rest of the world.

Africa

In comparison to the US and North America, nations in the global South such as those in Africa have significantly fewer airports. The people that live in these countries, especially in rural regions, are extremely isolated and lack easy access to global transportation.
